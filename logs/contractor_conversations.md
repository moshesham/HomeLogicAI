# Contractor Conversations Log

> **Purpose:** Record every significant conversation, claim, promise, warning, or price quoted by any contractor, supplier, or trade professional.
>
> **Why this matters:** A timestamped written record creates accountability. If a contractor later claims they "never said that" or that something "wasn't in scope," this log is your evidence.
>
> **How to use:** Add a new `##` entry for each conversation. Keep it factual. Include who said it, what they said, and any follow-up actions required.

---

## Log Format

```
## YYYY-MM-DD — [Name / Company / Trade]
**Location:** [Phone / On-site / Showroom / Email]
**Present:** [Who was in the conversation]

### What Was Said
- [Key claim, promise, or quote — verbatim if possible]
- [Another point]

### Follow-Up Required
- [ ] [Action item]
- [ ] [Verify X by Y date]

### Notes
[Any context, concerns, or gut-check observations]
```

---

## Example Entry

```
## 2024-03-15 — John Smith / ABC Electrical
**Location:** On-site walkthrough
**Present:** Homeowner, John Smith

### What Was Said
- Panel upgrade is NOT required for the ceiling fan circuits
- Quoted $350 for fan installation (covers 2 fans, same circuit)
- Warned that adding a NEW circuit (not a branch) requires a permit
- Said work could be completed in one day

### Follow-Up Required
- [ ] Get written quote by Friday 2024-03-22
- [ ] Verify permit requirement with local building department
- [ ] Confirm "same circuit" means no overload risk (request load calculation)

### Notes
John seemed confident but didn't have drawings with him.
Request load calculation in writing before signing anything.
```
